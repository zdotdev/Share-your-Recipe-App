Adobo: The National Dish:
Adobo, a beloved Filipino dish, is a flavorful stew made with meat (usually chicken or pork), vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves. The name “adobo” comes from the Spanish word “adobar,” meaning to marinate or season. It reflects the Spanish influence on Filipino cooking.

Sisig: From Pig’s Head to Delicacy:
Sisig is a sizzling dish made from chopped pig’s head, ears, and liver, seasoned with calamansi (Filipino lime), onions, and chili peppers.Originally a way to use leftover parts, sisig has transformed into a popular appetizer enjoyed with rice or beer.

Lechon: The King of Roasts:
Lechon is a whole roasted pig, crispy on the outside and tender inside. It’s a centerpiece at celebrations, fiestas, and special occasions. The skin is the prized part—crackling and golden brown. Lechon festivals celebrate this iconic dish.
